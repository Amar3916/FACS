Preventing a heart attack (myocardial infarction) and taking precautions until reaching the hospital are crucial steps that can save lives.

Here are the necessary steps and precautions:

Recognize the symptoms: Chest pain or discomfort, shortness of breath, cold sweats, nausea, and pain radiating to the arm or jaw are common symptoms of a heart attack. If you experience these, don't delay.

Call emergency services (911 or your local emergency number) immediately. Don't try to drive yourself to the hospital unless there's absolutely no other option.

Chew and swallow an aspirin (162-325 mg) if you're not allergic to it. Aspirin can help reduce blood clotting and improve blood flow.

Stop any activity and rest. Sit or lie down in a comfortable position.

If you have nitroglycerin prescribed for angina, take one dose as directed by your doctor.

Loosen any tight clothing around your chest and neck.

Stay calm and try to take slow, deep breaths.

If you're alone, unlock the door and wait for emergency services to arrive.

If you're with someone, have them stay with you until help arrives.

If the person stops breathing, begin CPR (if trained) until emergency services arrive.

Once emergency services arrive, follow their instructions carefully.

At the hospital, be prepared to provide information about your symptoms, medical history, and any medications you're taking.

Remember, prompt treatment is crucial for heart attack survival and minimizing damage to the heart muscle. Calling emergency services and getting to the hospital quickly can significantly improve your chances of a positive outcome.
