If choking occurs, follow these steps to assist the choking person:

1. *Assess the Severity*: Determine if the person is able to cough or speak. If they can cough forcefully, encourage them to continue coughing to try to dislodge the object. If they cannot cough, speak, or breathe, immediate action is needed.

2. *Call for Emergency Help*: If the person is choking and unable to breathe, speak, or cough effectively, call emergency services immediately or ask someone else to do so.

3. *Perform the Heimlich Maneuver (Abdominal Thrusts)*:

- Stand behind the choking person and wrap your arms around their waist.

- Make a fist with one hand and place it just above the navel, thumb side in.

- Grasp the fist with your other hand and give quick, upward thrusts into the abdomen, aiming to dislodge the object.

- Repeat abdominal thrusts until the object is expelled or the person becomes unconscious.

4. *Check the Mouth*: If the person becomes unconscious, carefully lay them on their back and check their mouth for any visible obstructions. If you see an object, carefully remove it with your fingers. Be cautious not to push the object further down the throat.

5. *Perform CPR (if Necessary)*:

- If the person becomes unconscious and unresponsive, and you suspect they are not breathing, begin CPR.

- Tilt the head back slightly and lift the chin to open the airway.

- Give two rescue breaths, ensuring the chest rises with each breath.

- Check for signs of circulation (such as breathing, coughing, or movement). If there are no signs of circulation, start chest compressions at a rate of 100 to 120 compressions per minute.

6. *Continue CPR and Monitor*: Continue CPR until emergency medical help arrives or until the person starts breathing on their own.

7. *Seek Medical Attention*: Even if the choking episode appears to resolve, it's advisable to seek medical attention to ensure there are no underlying issues or complications.

8. *Stay Calm and Reassure*: Throughout the process, remain calm and reassure the choking person to help keep them calm as well.

Remember, it's crucial to act quickly and confidently in a choking emergency, but also to prioritize your safety and the safety of others while providing assistance. If you're unsure about performing any of the steps, seek guidance from someone trained in first aid or wait for professional medical help to arrive.
